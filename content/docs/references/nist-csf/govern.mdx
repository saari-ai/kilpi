## GV.OC: Organizational Context

*The circumstances -- mission, stakeholder expectations, dependencies, and legal, regulatory, and contractual requirements -- surrounding the organization's cybersecurity risk management decisions are understood*

### Subcategories

| ID | Outcome |
|----|---------|
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.OC-01</span> | The organizational mission is understood and informs cybersecurity risk management |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.OC-02</span> | Internal and external stakeholders are understood, and their needs and expectations regarding cybersecurity risk management are understood and considered |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.OC-03</span> | Legal, regulatory, and contractual requirements regarding cybersecurity -- including privacy and civil liberties obligations -- are understood and managed |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.OC-04</span> | Critical objectives, capabilities, and services that external stakeholders depend on or expect from the organization are understood and communicated |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.OC-05</span> | Outcomes, capabilities, and services that the organization depends on are understood and communicated |

### Implementation examples

| ID | Example |
|----|---------|
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.OC-01 Ex1</span> | Share the organization's mission (e.g., through vision and mission statements, marketing, and service strategies) to provide a basis for identifying risks that may impede that mission |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.OC-02 Ex1</span> | Identify relevant internal stakeholders and their cybersecurity-related expectations (e.g., performance and risk expectations of officers, directors, and advisors; cultural expectations of employees) |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.OC-02 Ex2</span> | Identify relevant external stakeholders and their cybersecurity-related expectations (e.g., privacy expectations of customers, business expectations of partnerships, compliance expectations of regulators, ethics expectations of society) |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.OC-03 Ex1</span> | Determine a process to track and manage legal and regulatory requirements regarding protection of individuals' information (e.g., Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act, California Consumer Privacy Act, General Data Protection Regulation) |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.OC-03 Ex2</span> | Determine a process to track and manage contractual requirements for cybersecurity management of supplier, customer, and partner information |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.OC-03 Ex3</span> | Align the organization's cybersecurity strategy with legal, regulatory, and contractual requirements |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.OC-04 Ex1</span> | Establish criteria for determining the criticality of the organization's capabilities and services as viewed by internal and external stakeholders |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.OC-04 Ex2</span> | Determine (e.g., from a business impact analysis) assets and business operations that are vital to achieving mission objectives and the potential impact of a loss (or partial loss) of such operations |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.OC-04 Ex3</span> | Establish and communicate resilience objectives (e.g., recovery time objectives) for delivering critical capabilities and services in various operating states (e.g., under attack, during recovery, normal operation) |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.OC-05 Ex1</span> | Create an inventory of the organization's dependencies on external resources (e.g., facilities, cloud-based hosting providers) and their relationships to organizational assets and business functions |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.OC-05 Ex2</span> | Identify and document external dependencies that are potential points of failure for the organization's critical capabilities and services, and share that information with appropriate personnel |

## GV.RM: Risk Management Strategy

*The organization's priorities, constraints, risk tolerance and appetite statements, and assumptions are established, communicated, and used to support operational risk decisions*

### Subcategories

| ID | Outcome |
|----|---------|
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RM-01</span> | Risk management objectives are established and agreed to by organizational stakeholders |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RM-02</span> | Risk appetite and risk tolerance statements are established, communicated, and maintained |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RM-03</span> | Cybersecurity risk management activities and outcomes are included in enterprise risk management processes |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RM-04</span> | Strategic direction that describes appropriate risk response options is established and communicated |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RM-05</span> | Lines of communication across the organization are established for cybersecurity risks, including risks from suppliers and other third parties |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RM-06</span> | A standardized method for calculating, documenting, categorizing, and prioritizing cybersecurity risks is established and communicated |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RM-07</span> | Strategic opportunities (i.e., positive risks) are characterized and are included in organizational cybersecurity risk discussions |

### Implementation examples

| ID | Example |
|----|---------|
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RM-01 Ex1</span> | Update near-term and long-term cybersecurity risk management objectives as part of annual strategic planning and when major changes occur |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RM-01 Ex2</span> | Establish measurable objectives for cybersecurity risk management (e.g., manage the quality of user training, ensure adequate risk protection for industrial control systems) |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RM-01 Ex3</span> | Senior leaders agree about cybersecurity objectives and use them for measuring and managing risk and performance |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RM-02 Ex1</span> | Determine and communicate risk appetite statements that convey expectations about the appropriate level of risk for the organization |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RM-02 Ex2</span> | Translate risk appetite statements into specific, measurable, and broadly understandable risk tolerance statements |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RM-02 Ex3</span> | Refine organizational objectives and risk appetite periodically based on known risk exposure and residual risk |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RM-03 Ex1</span> | Aggregate and manage cybersecurity risks alongside other enterprise risks (e.g., compliance, financial, operational, regulatory, reputational, safety) |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RM-03 Ex2</span> | Include cybersecurity risk managers in enterprise risk management planning |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RM-03 Ex3</span> | Establish criteria for escalating cybersecurity risks within enterprise risk management |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RM-04 Ex1</span> | Specify criteria for accepting and avoiding cybersecurity risk for various classifications of data |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RM-04 Ex2</span> | Determine whether to purchase cybersecurity insurance |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RM-04 Ex3</span> | Document conditions under which shared responsibility models are acceptable (e.g., outsourcing certain cybersecurity functions, having a third party perform financial transactions on behalf of the organization, using public cloud-based services) |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RM-05 Ex1</span> | Determine how to update senior executives, directors, and management on the organization's cybersecurity posture at agreed-upon intervals |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RM-05 Ex2</span> | Identify how all departments across the organization -- such as management, operations, internal auditors, legal, acquisition, physical security, and HR -- will communicate with each other about cybersecurity risks |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RM-06 Ex1</span> | Establish criteria for using a quantitative approach to cybersecurity risk analysis, and specify probability and exposure formulas |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RM-06 Ex2</span> | Create and use templates (e.g., a risk register) to document cybersecurity risk information (e.g., risk description, exposure, treatment, and ownership) |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RM-06 Ex3</span> | Establish criteria for risk prioritization at the appropriate levels within the enterprise |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RM-06 Ex4</span> | Use a consistent list of risk categories to support integrating, aggregating, and comparing cybersecurity risks |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RM-07 Ex1</span> | Define and communicate guidance and methods for identifying opportunities and including them in risk discussions (e.g., strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats [SWOT] analysis) |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RM-07 Ex2</span> | Identify stretch goals and document them |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RM-07 Ex3</span> | Calculate, document, and prioritize positive risks alongside negative risks |

## GV.RR: Roles, Responsibilities, and Authorities

*Cybersecurity roles, responsibilities, and authorities to foster accountability, performance assessment, and continuous improvement are established and communicated*

### Subcategories

| ID | Outcome |
|----|---------|
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RR-01</span> | Organizational leadership is responsible and accountable for cybersecurity risk and fosters a culture that is risk-aware, ethical, and continually improving |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RR-02</span> | Roles, responsibilities, and authorities related to cybersecurity risk management are established, communicated, understood, and enforced |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RR-03</span> | Adequate resources are allocated commensurate with the cybersecurity risk strategy, roles, responsibilities, and policies |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RR-04</span> | Cybersecurity is included in human resources practices |

### Implementation examples

| ID | Example |
|----|---------|
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RR-01 Ex1</span> | Leaders (e.g., directors) agree on their roles and responsibilities in developing, implementing, and assessing the organization's cybersecurity strategy |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RR-01 Ex2</span> | Share leaders' expectations regarding a secure and ethical culture, especially when current events present the opportunity to highlight positive or negative examples of cybersecurity risk management |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RR-01 Ex3</span> | Leaders direct the CISO to maintain a comprehensive cybersecurity risk strategy and review and update it at least annually and after major events |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RR-01 Ex4</span> | Conduct reviews to ensure adequate authority and coordination among those responsible for managing cybersecurity risk |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RR-02 Ex1</span> | Document risk management roles and responsibilities in policy |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RR-02 Ex2</span> | Document who is responsible and accountable for cybersecurity risk management activities and how those teams and individuals are to be consulted and informed |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RR-02 Ex3</span> | Include cybersecurity responsibilities and performance requirements in personnel descriptions |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RR-02 Ex4</span> | Document performance goals for personnel with cybersecurity risk management responsibilities, and periodically measure performance to identify areas for improvement |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RR-02 Ex5</span> | Clearly articulate cybersecurity responsibilities within operations, risk functions, and internal audit functions |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RR-03 Ex1</span> | Conduct periodic management reviews to ensure that those given cybersecurity risk management responsibilities have the necessary authority |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RR-03 Ex2</span> | Identify resource allocation and investment in line with risk tolerance and response |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RR-03 Ex3</span> | Provide adequate and sufficient people, process, and technical resources to support the cybersecurity strategy |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RR-04 Ex1</span> | Integrate cybersecurity risk management considerations into human resources processes (e.g., personnel screening, onboarding, change notification, offboarding) |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RR-04 Ex2</span> | Consider cybersecurity knowledge to be a positive factor in hiring, training, and retention decisions |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RR-04 Ex3</span> | Conduct background checks prior to onboarding new personnel for sensitive roles, and periodically repeat background checks for personnel with such roles |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.RR-04 Ex4</span> | Define and enforce obligations for personnel to be aware of, adhere to, and uphold security policies as they relate to their roles |

## GV.PO: Policy

*Organizational cybersecurity policy is established, communicated, and enforced*

### Subcategories

| ID | Outcome |
|----|---------|
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.PO-01</span> | Policy for managing cybersecurity risks is established based on organizational context, cybersecurity strategy, and priorities and is communicated and enforced |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.PO-02</span> | Policy for managing cybersecurity risks is reviewed, updated, communicated, and enforced to reflect changes in requirements, threats, technology, and organizational mission |

### Implementation examples

| ID | Example |
|----|---------|
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.PO-01 Ex1</span> | Create, disseminate, and maintain an understandable, usable risk management policy with statements of management intent, expectations, and direction |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.PO-01 Ex2</span> | Periodically review policy and supporting processes and procedures to ensure that they align with risk management strategy objectives and priorities, as well as the high-level direction of the cybersecurity policy |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.PO-01 Ex3</span> | Require approval from senior management on policy |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.PO-01 Ex4</span> | Communicate cybersecurity risk management policy and supporting processes and procedures across the organization |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.PO-01 Ex5</span> | Require personnel to acknowledge receipt of policy when first hired, annually, and whenever policy is updated |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.PO-02 Ex1</span> | Update policy based on periodic reviews of cybersecurity risk management results to ensure that policy and supporting processes and procedures adequately maintain risk at an acceptable level |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.PO-02 Ex2</span> | Provide a timeline for reviewing changes to the organization's risk environment (e.g., changes in risk or in the organization's mission objectives), and communicate recommended policy updates |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.PO-02 Ex3</span> | Update policy to reflect changes in legal and regulatory requirements |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.PO-02 Ex4</span> | Update policy to reflect changes in technology (e.g., adoption of artificial intelligence) and changes to the business (e.g., acquisition of a new business, new contract requirements) |

## GV.OV: Oversight

*Results of organization-wide cybersecurity risk management activities and performance are used to inform, improve, and adjust the risk management strategy*

### Subcategories

| ID | Outcome |
|----|---------|
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.OV-01</span> | Cybersecurity risk management strategy outcomes are reviewed to inform and adjust strategy and direction |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.OV-02</span> | The cybersecurity risk management strategy is reviewed and adjusted to ensure coverage of organizational requirements and risks |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.OV-03</span> | Organizational cybersecurity risk management performance is evaluated and reviewed for adjustments needed |

### Implementation examples

| ID | Example |
|----|---------|
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.OV-01 Ex1</span> | Measure how well the risk management strategy and risk results have helped leaders make decisions and achieve organizational objectives |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.OV-01 Ex2</span> | Examine whether cybersecurity risk strategies that impede operations or innovation should be adjusted |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.OV-02 Ex1</span> | Review audit findings to confirm whether the existing cybersecurity strategy has ensured compliance with internal and external requirements |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.OV-02 Ex2</span> | Review the performance oversight of those in cybersecurity-related roles to determine whether policy changes are necessary |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.OV-02 Ex3</span> | Review strategy in light of cybersecurity incidents |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.OV-03 Ex1</span> | Review key performance indicators (KPIs) to ensure that organization-wide policies and procedures achieve objectives |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.OV-03 Ex2</span> | Review key risk indicators (KRIs) to identify risks the organization faces, including likelihood and potential impact |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.OV-03 Ex3</span> | Collect and communicate metrics on cybersecurity risk management with senior leadership |

## GV.SC: Cybersecurity Supply Chain Risk Management

*Cyber supply chain risk management processes are identified, established, managed, monitored, and improved by organizational stakeholders*

### Subcategories

| ID | Outcome |
|----|---------|
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-01</span> | A cybersecurity supply chain risk management program, strategy, objectives, policies, and processes are established and agreed to by organizational stakeholders |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-02</span> | Cybersecurity roles and responsibilities for suppliers, customers, and partners are established, communicated, and coordinated internally and externally |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-03</span> | Cybersecurity supply chain risk management is integrated into cybersecurity and enterprise risk management, risk assessment, and improvement processes |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-04</span> | Suppliers are known and prioritized by criticality |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-05</span> | Requirements to address cybersecurity risks in supply chains are established, prioritized, and integrated into contracts and other types of agreements with suppliers and other relevant third parties |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-06</span> | Planning and due diligence are performed to reduce risks before entering into formal supplier or other third-party relationships |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-07</span> | The risks posed by a supplier, their products and services, and other third parties are understood, recorded, prioritized, assessed, responded to, and monitored over the course of the relationship |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-08</span> | Relevant suppliers and other third parties are included in incident planning, response, and recovery activities |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-09</span> | Supply chain security practices are integrated into cybersecurity and enterprise risk management programs, and their performance is monitored throughout the technology product and service life cycle |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-10</span> | Cybersecurity supply chain risk management plans include provisions for activities that occur after the conclusion of a partnership or service agreement |

### Implementation examples

| ID | Example |
|----|---------|
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-01 Ex1</span> | Establish a strategy that expresses the objectives of the cybersecurity supply chain risk management program |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-01 Ex2</span> | Develop the cybersecurity supply chain risk management program, including a plan (with milestones), policies, and procedures that guide implementation and improvement of the program, and share the policies and procedures with the organizational stakeholders |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-01 Ex3</span> | Develop and implement program processes based on the strategy, objectives, policies, and procedures that are agreed upon and performed by the organizational stakeholders |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-01 Ex4</span> | Establish a cross-organizational mechanism that ensures alignment between functions that contribute to cybersecurity supply chain risk management, such as cybersecurity, IT, operations, legal, human resources, and engineering |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-02 Ex1</span> | Identify one or more specific roles or positions that will be responsible and accountable for planning, resourcing, and executing cybersecurity supply chain risk management activities |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-02 Ex2</span> | Document cybersecurity supply chain risk management roles and responsibilities in policy |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-02 Ex3</span> | Create responsibility matrixes to document who will be responsible and accountable for cybersecurity supply chain risk management activities and how those teams and individuals will be consulted and informed |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-02 Ex4</span> | Include cybersecurity supply chain risk management responsibilities and performance requirements in personnel descriptions to ensure clarity and improve accountability |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-02 Ex5</span> | Document performance goals for personnel with cybersecurity risk management-specific responsibilities, and periodically measure them to demonstrate and improve performance |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-02 Ex6</span> | Develop roles and responsibilities for suppliers, customers, and business partners to address shared responsibilities for applicable cybersecurity risks, and integrate them into organizational policies and applicable third-party agreements |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-02 Ex7</span> | Internally communicate cybersecurity supply chain risk management roles and responsibilities for third parties |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-02 Ex8</span> | Establish rules and protocols for information sharing and reporting processes between the organization and its suppliers |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-03 Ex1</span> | Identify areas of alignment and overlap with cybersecurity and enterprise risk management |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-03 Ex2</span> | Establish integrated control sets for cybersecurity risk management and cybersecurity supply chain risk management |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-03 Ex3</span> | Integrate cybersecurity supply chain risk management into improvement processes |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-03 Ex4</span> | Escalate material cybersecurity risks in supply chains to senior management, and address them at the enterprise risk management level |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-04 Ex1</span> | Develop criteria for supplier criticality based on, for example, the sensitivity of data processed or possessed by suppliers, the degree of access to the organization's systems, and the importance of the products or services to the organization's mission |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-04 Ex2</span> | Keep a record of all suppliers, and prioritize suppliers based on the criticality criteria |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-05 Ex1</span> | Establish security requirements for suppliers, products, and services commensurate with their criticality level and potential impact if compromised |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-05 Ex2</span> | Include all cybersecurity and supply chain requirements that third parties must follow and how compliance with the requirements may be verified in default contractual language |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-05 Ex3</span> | Define the rules and protocols for information sharing between the organization and its suppliers and sub-tier suppliers in agreements |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-05 Ex4</span> | Manage risk by including security requirements in agreements based on their criticality and potential impact if compromised |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-05 Ex5</span> | Define security requirements in service-level agreements (SLAs) for monitoring suppliers for acceptable security performance throughout the supplier relationship lifecycle |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-05 Ex6</span> | Contractually require suppliers to disclose cybersecurity features, functions, and vulnerabilities of their products and services for the life of the product or the term of service |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-05 Ex7</span> | Contractually require suppliers to provide and maintain a current component inventory (e.g., software or hardware bill of materials) for critical products |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-05 Ex8</span> | Contractually require suppliers to vet their employees and guard against insider threats |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-05 Ex9</span> | Contractually require suppliers to provide evidence of performing acceptable security practices through, for example, self-attestation, conformance to known standards, certifications, or inspections |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-05 Ex10</span> | Specify in contracts and other agreements the rights and responsibilities of the organization, its suppliers, and their supply chains, with respect to potential cybersecurity risks |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-06 Ex1</span> | Perform thorough due diligence on prospective suppliers that is consistent with procurement planning and commensurate with the level of risk, criticality, and complexity of each supplier relationship |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-06 Ex2</span> | Assess the suitability of the technology and cybersecurity capabilities and the risk management practices of prospective suppliers |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-06 Ex3</span> | Conduct supplier risk assessments against business and applicable cybersecurity requirements |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-06 Ex4</span> | Assess the authenticity, integrity, and security of critical products prior to acquisition and use |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-07 Ex1</span> | Adjust assessment formats and frequencies based on the third party's reputation and the criticality of the products or services they provide |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-07 Ex2</span> | Evaluate third parties' evidence of compliance with contractual cybersecurity requirements, such as self-attestations, warranties, certifications, and other artifacts |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-07 Ex3</span> | Monitor critical suppliers to ensure that they are fulfilling their security obligations throughout the supplier relationship lifecycle using a variety of methods and techniques, such as inspections, audits, tests, or other forms of evaluation |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-07 Ex4</span> | Monitor critical suppliers, services, and products for changes to their risk profiles, and reevaluate supplier criticality and risk impact accordingly |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-07 Ex5</span> | Plan for unexpected supplier and supply chain-related interruptions to ensure business continuity |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-08 Ex1</span> | Define and use rules and protocols for reporting incident response and recovery activities and the status between the organization and its suppliers |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-08 Ex2</span> | Identify and document the roles and responsibilities of the organization and its suppliers for incident response |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-08 Ex3</span> | Include critical suppliers in incident response exercises and simulations |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-08 Ex4</span> | Define and coordinate crisis communication methods and protocols between the organization and its critical suppliers |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-08 Ex5</span> | Conduct collaborative lessons learned sessions with critical suppliers |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-09 Ex1</span> | Policies and procedures require provenance records for all acquired technology products and services |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-09 Ex2</span> | Periodically provide risk reporting to leaders about how acquired components are proven to be untampered and authentic |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-09 Ex3</span> | Communicate regularly among cybersecurity risk managers and operations personnel about the need to acquire software patches, updates, and upgrades only from authenticated and trustworthy software providers |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-09 Ex4</span> | Review policies to ensure that they require approved supplier personnel to perform maintenance on supplier products |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-09 Ex5</span> | Policies and procedures require checking upgrades to critical hardware for unauthorized changes |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-10 Ex1</span> | Establish processes for terminating critical relationships under both normal and adverse circumstances |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-10 Ex2</span> | Define and implement plans for component end-of-life maintenance support and obsolescence |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-10 Ex3</span> | Verify that supplier access to organization resources is deactivated promptly when it is no longer needed |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-10 Ex4</span> | Verify that assets containing the organization's data are returned or properly disposed of in a timely, controlled, and safe manner |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-10 Ex5</span> | Develop and execute a plan for terminating or transitioning supplier relationships that takes supply chain security risk and resiliency into account |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-10 Ex6</span> | Mitigate risks to data and systems created by supplier termination |
| <span style={{whiteSpace: 'nowrap'}}>GV.SC-10 Ex7</span> | Manage data leakage risks associated with supplier termination |
